What is ROI?

Return on Investment (ROI) is a performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment. It compares the profit or loss relative to the initial cost.

The ROI Formula

The formula is short and memorable:

r = (F / P) ^ (1 / t) - 1

  • F — final value (or total amount returned)
  • P — principal (the starting amount)
  • r — annual interest rate, as a decimal (so 6% becomes 0.06)
  • t — time in years

A Worked Example

Suppose your starting amount is $5,000, and you have $7,000 after 3 years of investment. Plugging into the formula:

r = (7000/5000)^(1/3)-1 = 11.87%

Another example: If you invest $1,000 and it grows to $1,500, your ROI is 50%.

Tips for Using This Calculator

  • Enter the Principal Amount greater than 0.
  • For sub-year periods, use a fractional year. 6 months is 0.5, 90 days is roughly 0.25.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ROI the same as profit?

No, ROI is a percentage, while profit is an absolute value.

What is a good ROI?

A good ROI depends on the investment type, but generally 10%+ is considered strong.

Can I use this for monthly or daily ROI?

Yes — convert your time period to years. Six months is 0.5; 90 days is ~0.25. It should be noted that the minimum input time is 0.25.
